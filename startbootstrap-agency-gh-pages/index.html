History of SHPE

Nuestra Historia

  • 1974

    Our Humble Beginnings

    The Society of Hispanic Professional Engineers was founded in Los Angeles, California in 1974 by a group of engineers employed by the city of Los Angeles. Their objective was to form a national organization of professional engineers to serve as role models in the Hispanic community.

  • 1988

    SHPE Comes to Stevens

    SHPE at Stevens Institute of Technology was founded by Stevens' student, Hermes Gonzalez. Hermes established the chapter to provide a support system for Hispanics on campus.

  • December 1991

    Official Recognition

    In December of 1991, the chapter was officially recognized by SHPE Inc., the national governing body. SHPE@SIT has instituted a much-needed support system for Hispanic students in engineering and science. It also has been successful in its efforts to reach out and have a positive impact on young Hispanics within the surrounding communities.

  • December 2016

    SHPE@SIT Garners National Recognition

    In 2016, the chapter received the Outstanding Medium-Sized Chapter of the Year award at the annual SHPE conference held in Seattle, Washington. The award is presented to a chapter comprising 26 to 75 members based on the strength and success of the chapter's programs in all event categories: academic development, professional development, leadership development, community outreach, and chapter development.
